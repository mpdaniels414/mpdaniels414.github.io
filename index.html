I am a Data Scientist and Machine Learning Engineer conducting research in systems engineering and data integration optimization using advanced statistical and machine learning methods. At Carnegie Mellon University's Software Engineering Institute, I develop quantitative models that support cost estimation, performance evaluation, and risk analysis for large-scale cyber-physical and software-intensive systems.

Previously, I worked at W.R. Berkley Corporation and Deloitte, where I led analytics initiatives, developed predictive models, and helped organizations implement data-driven strategies. Earlier in my career at New York Life, I gained experience in actuarial science, risk modeling, and insurance analytics.

I hold a Master’s degree in Data Science from the Georgia Tech and a Bachelor’s degree in Applied Mathematics from the University of Georgia.

Before anyone asks about potential dual loyalty between Georgia and Georgia Tech, I’m a die-hard Georgia fan; you will catch me dead before you find me in gold and blue.

Outside of my work in mathematics, statistics, and computer science, I’m also a rock climber, lacrosse player, and avid outdoorsman.
